Problem

Classification

Will an ICE stop be delayed ≥ 6 min?

Binary prediction: delayed or on-time. Threshold aligns with Deutsche Bahn's significant-delay reporting.

Metrics: F1, ROC-AUC, Confusion Matrix

Regression

How many minutes late?

Continuous prediction. MAE is interpretable in minutes; RMSE penalizes large errors (severe delays).

Metrics: MAE, RMSE, R²

Why both? Operators need to know whether to trigger mitigations and how much to inform passengers — two sides of the same operational need.

Key Design Decisions

Time-based split?

Random split causes temporal leakage. Time-split reflects real deployment: predict future from past.

6-minute threshold?

Aligned with DB's "significant delay." 5 min too noisy; 15 min too few positives.

Drop change times?

Actual times (arrival_change_time) encode the delay. Using them leaks the target.

Parquet + streaming?

54.8 GB. Parquet is columnar + compressed. 100K-row chunks keep memory low.

Only ICE?

High-profile long-distance. Removing other types avoids confounding from different profiles.

July only?

Proof-of-concept shard for fast iteration. Pipeline parameterized for any month.

Why Weak Weather Correlations?

Pearson r between weather variables and delay is < 0.15 for all 8 variables.

Non-linear Effects

Delay rate jumps from 41.6% (dry) to 44.5% (wet), yet r ≈ 0. The relationship is a step function, not linear.

Tree Models Help

Random Forest, XGBoost capture non-linear interactions naturally. Planned for Notebook 08.

Ablation Will Tell

RQ2 compares identical models ± weather. Even with low r, weather may improve via non-linear paths.

July Factor

Summer is mild. Winter (snow, ice) may show stronger correlations. Pipeline handles more months.
